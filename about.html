About the Course

The course will provide an overview of the American Educational system as an institution. Students will learn and apply sociological theories of education, evaluate research on education, understand the role of education in social reproduction as well as social dynamics and change, and develop awareness of how education affects their own lives.

Upon completion of the course students will:

  • Use the sociological imagination to describe and explain the role of education and the educational system in his or her own life;
  • Understand and apply sociological concepts and theories to interpret and assess educational outcomes in different cultural and social structural settings;
  • Describe, compare, contrast, and apply theoretical orientations in the sociology of education;
  • Analyze the effects of variations in culture and social structure on educational outcomes;
  • Provide examples that illustrate the impact of race, class, and gender on educational outcomes;
  • Summarize, critically assess, and synthesize research reports in the sociology of education;
  • Create a final report that integrates an important research question, quantitative or qualitative data, and policy implications; and Revise and rewrite for deeper understanding and clarity of communication on issues in the sociology of education.

Program Learning Outcomes addressed by the course:

  • Self-Awareness and Ethics
  • Information Literacy & Information Evaluation
  • Theory: Application, Evaluation, Testing
  • Basic Sociological Concepts
  • Critical Thinking
  • Written Communication
